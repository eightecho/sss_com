About the Author

TADROBERT

Writer. Strategist. Creative. Builder — currently in public.

I've spent thirty years in advertising, marketing, and independent work as a freelancer and consultant — and about ten of those years trying to turn everything I learned into a system that actually makes sense.

My career started in advertising agencies in New York, building brand campaigns for companies like Quaker Oats, Novartis, and Hasbro. I moved into tech startups during the first internet wave, recruited as Creative Director to build a marketing communications department from scratch. Then a corporate stint managing channel marketing for a major satellite brand. Then, since 2005, independent consulting — working with everyone from early-stage startups to established companies on marketing, communications, and strategy.

Somewhere in the middle of all that, I built something of my own — a self-funded SaaS product and a small team to deliver event marketing automation software. We built it, used it on client work, and were preparing to open it to the public. Then the 2008 market crash hit and shut us down.

It was a convoluted, expensive, and clarifying experience. We had the product. We had the clients. We had the intent. What we didn't have was a system — a clear, sequential process for building and launching something sustainable without the chaos, the wasted effort, and the decisions made in the wrong order at the wrong time.

That experience set me down the path of Stupid Simple Startup. The IMPACT System is what I wish I'd had.

Building in Public

I built the IMPACT System for myself — and for every Side Hustler, Solopreneur, and Subject Matter Expert building real things with limited time, limited resources, and no room for the kind of mistakes that come from not having a complete system.

I'm building my first solo Stupid Simple Startup in public right now — a picture book project. Which means you're not just getting a framework. You're getting a front-row seat to the process of building something real, making decisions with incomplete information, and improving systematically over time. The same loop this book teaches.

Why This Book Is Free

The IMPACT System is intentionally free. Not as a loss leader. Not as a marketing funnel disguised as generosity. The system belongs to anyone willing to use it — because a system that helps people build real businesses is more valuable out in the world than locked behind a paywall.
