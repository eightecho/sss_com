Phase 1 of 2

Build the Business Foundation

Before you can sell anything, you must have something worth selling to someone who wants it at a price that sustains you. This sounds obvious. Yet most business failures happen here — not in execution, not in marketing — but in the foundation work that most people rush or skip entirely.

01

Phase 1 Answers Three Questions

Should this exist, and for whom? Identify

Not what you could build or what you think people need — what specific people actually struggle with enough to pay you to solve it. The customer, the problem, the competitive context, the positioning. Everything downstream depends on getting this right.

Can this make money, and how? Monetize

Not projected optimism — real economics with actual costs, defensible pricing, and positive unit economics. You need to know how each sale generates profit before you build anything.

What exactly are we selling right now? Productize

Not the complete vision or version 5.0 — the minimum viable version that delivers the promised value at the established price. The thing a paying customer could receive tomorrow.

* * *
02

The Phase 1 Discipline

The most common Phase 1 mistake: three days on foundation, six months on marketing — then wondering why nothing converts. Beautiful execution for an offer nobody wants, at a price that doesn't work, delivered through a product that overpromises and underdelivers.

The IMPACT discipline: get Phase 1 right. Not perfect — right. Clear customer, viable economics, complete product. Everything in Phase 2 depends on it.

Phase 1 is complete when you can deliver your product to a paying customer tomorrow and the transaction is sound — for both of you. If you can't do that yet, you're not ready for Phase 2. The foundation determines everything that follows.
