What they are

Deliverables, not exercises.

Each worksheet is built for one step of the IMPACT System. You work through it alongside the playbook chapter — and come out the other side with something real: a validated niche, a revenue model, a production plan.

Structured

Not blank pages

Every worksheet has specific prompts, frameworks, and decision points. You're not starting from scratch — you're filling in the right blanks.

Step-specific

One per chapter

Each worksheet pairs with one chapter of the playbook — applied to your specific business, not a generic template you could find anywhere.

Output-focused

Something to show for it

You finish each worksheet with a concrete deliverable — a decision made, a plan documented, a next step confirmed before you move on.

Inside the playbook

Six steps. Six worksheets.

Worksheets are included in the Picture Book Project Playbook — one for each chapter of the IMPACT System.

Step 1

Identify

Validate your niche, map the market, and lock in your publishing angle — before committing time, money, or energy to the wrong thing.

Step 2

Monetize

Build a real revenue model before you make a single page. Pricing, distribution channels, and projected returns — documented and defensible.

Step 3

Productize

Plan the actual production of the book — manuscript, illustration brief, file specs, and print-on-demand setup — step by step.

Step 4

Activate

Map out your first-sales plan before the book ships. Pre-sale outreach, early reader strategy, and the micro-launch framework.

Step 5

Campaign

Design the ongoing sales engine — Amazon listing optimization, content strategy, email list, and paid promotion planning.

Step 6

Triumph

Review the full cycle — revenue vs. plan, what worked, what to cut, and a documented decision about what comes next.

How they fit

"Part of the Playbook,
not separate from it."

Worksheets aren't a bonus you get after you finish reading. They're woven into each chapter — meant to be worked through in parallel with the training. The playbook tells you what decisions need to be made. The worksheets are how you actually make them.
